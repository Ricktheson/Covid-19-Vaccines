AstraZeneca vaccine




AstraZeneca Pharmaceutical Company (plc) Vaccine

AztraZeneca plc is a British-Swedish multinational pharmaceutical and biotechnology company with its headquarters at the Cambridge Biomedical Campus in Cambridge, England. It is perhaps best known for its involvement in developing the Oxford-Astrazeneca COVID-19 vaccine. The company was founded in 1999 through the merger of the Swedish Astra AB and the British Zeneca Group. Since the merger it has been among the world's largest pharmaceutical companies.

The AstraZeneca vaccine against COVID-19 has an efficiency of 51% to 72% based on trial results in the United Kingdom, Brazil and South Africa.



General Information about AstraZeneca vaccine

AstraZeneca is an inactivated virus vaccine to prevent the spread of Covid-19.

  • Name: AZD1222
  • Manufacturer: AztraZeneca
  • Type of vaccine: Inactivated virus
  • Number of shots: 2 shots, 8 to 12 weeks apart
  • How given: Shot in deltoid muscle
  • Does not contain:
    • Animal driven product
    • Restricted material
  • Side Effects:
    • Pain or tenderness at the injection site
    • Headache
    • Tiredness
    • Muscle or joint aches
    • Fever
    • Chills
    • Nausea
  • Age indications: 18 years of age and older
