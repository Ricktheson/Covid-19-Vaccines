Sinovac CoronaVac vaccine




CoronaVac Vaccine

In early 2020, the Chinese pharmaceutical company Sinovac initiated development in a potential COVID-19 vaccine, with initial Phase I-II clinical trials performed in China by May on the elderly, and by September on the youth. Phase III clinical trials proceeded on a global level from September onwards, with trials run in Latin America, Europe and Asia. Concluding the Phase III trials with encouraging results, the CoronaVac vaccine began to be produced for commercial distribution by Sinovac. At time of writing, it is currently in full authorisation for distribution in China, and is in immediate authorisation for use in countries such as Malaysia, Brazil, Mexico and Zimbabwe.

Current field results have yielded mixed results for overall efficacy of the vaccine, with success rates ranging from 50.4% to 91.25%.



General Information about the CoronaVac Vaccine

The CoronaVac vaccine is an inactivated virus vaccine to prevent the spread of Covid-19.

Below are the information about the vaccine.

  • Name: CoronaVac
  • Manufacturer: Sinovac Biotech Ltd [北京科兴生物制品有限公司]
  • Type of vaccine: Inactivated virus
  • Dosage: 2 shots, 14 days apart
  • Method of delivery: Intramuscular injection in the upper arm
  • Does not contain:
    • Eggs
    • Preservatives
    • Latex
  • Side Effects:
    • Pain
    • Redness
    • Swelling
    • Tiredness
    • Headache
    • Muscle pain at injection site
    • Chills
    • Fever
    • Diarrhoea
  • Age indications: 12 years of age and older

CoronaVac Transport and Storage

The CoronaVac vaccine can be stored at a temperature between 2°C and 8°C, reducing the need for refrigeration and additional infrastructure. The vaccine can be stored at these temperatures for three years in storage.
