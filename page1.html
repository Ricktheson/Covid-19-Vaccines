Pfizer vaccine




Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 Vaccine

On December 11, 2020, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration issued the first emergency use authorization (EUA) for a vaccine for the prevention of coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) in individuals 16 years of age and older. The emergency use authorization allows the Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 Vaccine to be distributed in the U.S.

Based on evidence from clinical trials, the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine was 95% effective at preventing laboratory-confirmed COVID-19 illness in people without evidence of previous infection.



General Information about Pfizer Vaccine

Pfizer is an mRNA vaccine to prevent the spread of Covid-19.

Below are the information about the vaccine.

  • Name: BNT162b2
  • Manufacturer: Pfizer, Inc., and BioNTech
  • Type of vaccine: mRNA
  • Number of shots: 2 shots, 21 days apart
  • How given: Shot in the muscle of the upper arm
  • Does not contain:
    • Eggs
    • Preservatives
    • Latex
  • Side Effects:
    • Pain
    • Redness
    • Swelling
    • Tiredness
    • Headache
    • Muscle pain
    • Chills
    • Fever
    • Nausea
  • Age indications: 16 years of age and older

Below shows how to store the vaccine

Vaccine will arrive at a temperature between -80°C and -60°C (-112°F to -76°F) in a thermal shipping container with dry ice. Diluent and an ancillary supply kits will arrive separately from the vaccine. Unpack the thermal shipping container following the manufacturer’s directionsexternal icon.
